01 — Overview

The Short Version

The Accountability Challenge Co. ("TAC Co.", "we", "us", or "our") operates theaccountabilityco.com and its associated challenge sites: joinsmac.com, joinwac.com, and jointheaac.com (collectively, "the Sites").

We collect only what we need to run the challenges, communicate with members, and process payments. We don't sell your data. We don't run ads. We use a small number of trusted third-party tools to operate the Sites.

Plain language commitment: This policy is written to be read, not buried. If something here is unclear, email us at [email protected] and we'll clarify it.

02 — Information We Collect

What We Collect

We collect information in three ways: what you give us directly, what's collected automatically when you use the Sites, and what comes from third-party tools we use to operate.

Information you provide directly:

  • Name and email address when you join a waitlist, sign up for a challenge, or contact us
  • Payment information processed by Stripe (we do not store card details — see Payments section)
  • Submission content such as screenshots, links, or form responses you submit as part of a challenge
  • Messages sent to us via contact forms or email

Information collected automatically:

  • Browser type, device type, and operating system
  • Pages visited, time on site, and referring URLs
  • IP address and general location data
03 — How We Use It

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Operate and administer the challenges (SMAC, WAC, AAC)
  • Process your enrollment and payments
  • Send challenge-related communications — updates, submission reminders, results
  • Send marketing emails to opted-in subscribers (unsubscribe anytime)
  • Respond to support requests and contact form submissions
  • Improve the Sites and challenge experience over time
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any automated decision-making that affects your eligibility in a challenge.

04 — Sharing & Disclosure

Who We Share Data With

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We share data only with the third-party services necessary to operate the Sites:

  • Stripe — payment processing. Stripe's privacy policy governs how they handle payment data.
  • MailerLite — email list management and campaign delivery for opted-in subscribers.
  • Formspree — contact form submission routing.
  • Hosting providers — the infrastructure that serves the Sites (e.g., GitHub Pages, Netlify, or similar).

We may disclose your information if required by law, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of TAC Co. or its members.

05 — Email Communications

Emails & Marketing

By joining a waitlist or subscribing via a form on the Sites, you agree to receive email communications from TAC Co. These may include challenge announcements, enrollment reminders, round updates, and promotional content.

You can unsubscribe at any time using the link at the bottom of any email, or by emailing us directly. Transactional emails related to an active enrollment (receipts, submission confirmations) are not subject to unsubscribe and are sent as part of your membership.

We use MailerLite to manage email lists. Your email address is stored in their system. See MailerLite's Privacy Policy for details.

06 — Payments

Payment Processing

All payments are processed by Stripe. TAC Co. does not store, handle, or have access to your credit card number, CVV, or full payment details. When you complete a purchase, you are submitting payment information directly to Stripe's secure infrastructure.

Stripe may collect additional data as part of their fraud prevention and payment processing services. See Stripe's Privacy Policy for full details.

We retain a record of your transaction (amount, date, product purchased) for accounting and support purposes.

07 — Cookies

Cookies & Tracking

The Sites may use cookies and similar technologies for basic functionality and analytics. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking pixels for retargeting.

Third-party tools embedded on the Sites (Stripe, MailerLite) may set their own cookies as part of their standard operation. You can configure your browser to block or delete cookies, though this may affect some site functionality.

We do not currently use a cookie consent banner, but will update this policy if our cookie usage changes materially.

08 — Your Rights

Your Rights & Choices

Depending on where you're located, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Delete your personal data (subject to any legal retention requirements)
  • Opt out of marketing emails at any time
  • Data portability — receive a copy of your data in a common format

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at [email protected]. We'll respond within 30 days.

Note: Deleting your account or data does not entitle you to a refund of any challenge entry fees already paid.

09 — Children

Children's Privacy

The Sites and challenges are intended for adults aged 18 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has submitted information to us, contact us immediately and we will delete it.

10 — Changes

Policy Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. For material changes, we'll notify active members via email. Continued use of the Sites after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

We recommend checking this page periodically if you're an active member of any TAC Co. challenge.
